Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

If you sign up to my website care plan, I will carry out monthly backups and monitor the security of the website.

If you have a website care plan

If you sign up to my website care plan, I will give you editor rights to your website so that you can edit the content, but nothing you do should break the website. I will be responsible for updates and maintenance.

For the 30 days after the launch, I will fix any bugs found on the website or any issues caused by updates. After that time, I will supply you with either some next steps (e.g. contact your hosting provider) or a quote for fixing the website.

If you don’t have a website care plan

If you don’t sign up to my website care plan, I give you admin rights to the website. Having admin rights means you are responsible for updates and maintenance, and you have access to all the website settings. So if the website breaks, we have no way of knowing if it was a mistake during the website build or something later that caused it.

If the website breaks and you have not signed up for my website care plan, your first step would be to contact your hosting provider. If they tell you to contact your website developer, you can email me and I will supply you with a quote for fixing the website.

At the end of the project, I provide my clients with a list of online resources to use when using their website. I also provide one-to-one training sessions as an optional extra.

If you have any questions in the 30 days after my training session, you can email me. I will provide you with up to an hour of support after my training session (if that is used up, I will quote my hourly rate to provide you with support).

If your project did not include a training session, you can email me and I will quote my hourly rate to provide you with support.

The first step is to get your new website or web page indexed by Google. There are many factors that determine how quick Google indexes it. It can take as little as a few days and up to six months for Google to crawl a site and attribute authority to the domain.

Indexing your website doesn’t mean it shows up at the top of the first page of the search results for your desired term. There are many factors that determine what Google displays. And limited spots are available spots on page one.

I outline what methods I use for search engine optimisation (SEO) in my website proposal/quote. I also outline what is not included.

There are plenty of free resources to find out more about SEO. Here's information from Google: https://developers.google.com/search/docs/beginner/get-started
