Where to get images for your business’ website

Stock photos

Stock images are images that are supplied for public use under a license. Using stock photography can be a good way to reduce your website project’s budget and timescale.

You can get good stock photos that your business can use for free from unsplash.com. If you need something more specific, shutterstock.com has a range of photos and illustrations at a low cost. (By the way, I’m not associated with either Unsplash or Shutterstock in any way.)

Tips for using stock images:

  • Look for realism. A typical cheesy stock image has a group of unrealistically attractive models pointing at a computer screen and laughing. When was the last time that happened in your office?
  • Check the licence for each image you download to make sure it covers what you want to use it for. If you found an image using Google Search, you probably don’t have the right to use it.
  • You can see if one of your competitors is already using the image online with a reverse image search using Google Search (instructions here).

You might not have time to get your website images yourself. Or you might feel that the task is not part of your skillset. If that’s the case, I’m happy to help. Taking photos on a smartphone

Thanks to the latest mobile devices, we can take high-quality photos without using extra equipment.

Tips for taking photos on a smartphone:

  • Turn off flash and pick the brightest time of day so you have more natural light.
  • Hold your phone straight and parallel to the floor.
  • Take a mixture of landscape and portrait shots.
  • Use both hands to keep your hands steady and help you take a clear image.
  • Focus on one subject – don’t have too much going on in one shot.
  • Avoid zooming in – try to get closer to your subject instead.

Hire a photographer

My favourite type of website project is one where I work with a photographer to create the images. If you want to hire the photographer yourself, it’s good to keep in mind that photographers have different specialities, e.g., portraits, architecture, events, product photography, or food photography.

What to do before the photoshoot:

  • Create a list of the photos you need; you don’t want to realise afterwards that you’re missing something.
  • If they’re photographing your premises, make sure everything is clean and tidy and let your staff know what they should wear.
  • Get permission to use the photos from any people being photographed.
