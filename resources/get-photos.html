Where to get photos for your website

As the online world is increasingly visual, it’s important that your website content includes images. Here are three ways to get images for your business’ website.

Get stock photos

Stock images are supplied for public use under a license. Using stock photography can be a good way to reduce your website project’s budget and timescale.

You can get stock photos that your business can use for free from unsplash.com. If you need something more specific, shutterstock.com has a range of photos and illustrations at a low cost. (By the way, I’m not associated with either Unsplash or Shutterstock in any way.)

Tips for using stock images:

  • Check the licence for each image you download to make sure it covers what you want to use it for.
  • See if one of your competitors is already using the image online with a reverse image search in Google Search (instructions here).

For more tips, see my post about how to choose the right images for your website.

I’m happy to help if you don’t have time to get stock images yourself (or if you feel that it’s not your forte). I don’t usually include creating or sourcing images as part of my website package, because a lot of clients have images already. So let me know if you’d like me to add this service to your project.

Take photos on a smartphone

Thanks to the latest mobile devices, we can take high-quality photos without using extra equipment.

Tips for taking photos on a smartphone:

  • Turn off the flash and pick the brightest time of day so you have more natural light.
  • Hold your phone straight and parallel to the floor.
  • Take a mixture of landscape and portrait shots.
  • Use both hands to keep your hands steady and help you take a clear image.
  • Focus on one subject – don’t have too much going on in one shot.
  • Avoid zooming in – try to get closer to your subject instead.

More tips:

  • Phone photography 101 from Hubspot ›

Hire a photographer

My favourite type of website project is one where I work with a photographer to create the images.

If you want to hire the photographer yourself, it’s good to keep in mind that photographers have different specialities, e.g., portraits, architecture, events, product photography, or food photography.

What to do before the photoshoot:

  • Create a list of the photos you need – you don’t want to realise afterwards that you’re missing something.
  • If they’re photographing your premises, make sure everything is clean and tidy and let your staff know what they should wear.
  • Get permission to use the photos from any people being photographed.
