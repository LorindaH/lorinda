What to replace an image carousel with

It’s tempting to show several images in a slider or carousel. But there is plenty of evidence that sliders and carousels are not helpful to your website visitors. (For more information, there’s this article by marketing experts CXL and this one by Yoast SEO.)

Your website visitor doesn’t have the patience to sit and wait for the next slide, so they’ll only see one of your slides. A set of images in a carousel may collectively give an accurate impression of what you do. But what happens if the one image they see when they land on the page is the one that’s wrong for them?

So what do we do instead? It’s not enough for me to say that you have to pick one offer. Most business coaches or marketing experts will tell you that you have to pick a niche, but that’s a topic for another time. For now, let’s look at what we can use instead of a slider or carousel.

Add small images to your main image

You may have one main offer and you don’t want to exclude your other offers. In this case, you can show one big image then add a few small images just underneath it.

For example, when I created a website for a Volkswagen show, I chose a photo of their biggest display for the main hero image (their historic Volkswagen Beetle display). This image draws attention to the text because the cars are facing the text box.

The client suggested having a slider because they wanted to show that they have a variety of cars on display. Instead, I included images of other models just underneath the main image (a new Beetle model and an 80s Volkswagen Golf).

Split up the main image

If you have several very distinct target markets, you can split the main image. Link each section to a page that is for that type of customer.

When I created a website for an electrical contractor, I created an option where the main image was split into the three property types they worked in (commercial, industrial, and domestic).

When I created a website for a company that sold machinery, I skipped straight to the product category section.

Use a collage

Another way to show a variety of offers is to create a collage out of different images.

When I created a website for a back and joint clinic, I created a collage to show back, knee and shoulder pain.
